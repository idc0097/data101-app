Where do ocean plastic pollution come from?

Plastic pollution is one of the world's major environmental issues. It encompasses the harmful and excessive accumulation of plastic objects and particles across wildlife, terrains, and bodies of water. Due to plastic's polymeric material, it is difficult for the environment to naturally degrade it. Hence, plastics may take centuries to fully reach decay. In our oceans, plastic pollution has become a threat to marine life, where wildlife species devour microplastic fragments. Whether it is for saving the sustainability of our oceans or fears of hazardous consumption of sea life, ocean plastic pollution has shown disturbing data that requires solutions from all sectors.

Now what?

Plastic has become ingrained in our daily living. Majority of the human population has depended on plastic, the accompaniment of modern life. Single-use plastics has made living 'convenient', yet slowly fills our oceans for centuries. This visualization will guide you through the plastic generation, its history in data, and how it continues to live and kill through our oceans.

Global Plastic Production fom 1950 to 2015

Since 1950, plastic production have continually increased annually. At an initial number of nearly 2 million tonnes per year, the plastic production have increased up to 381 million in 2015. This slow-burn rapid increase of plastic production has provided the world a catastrophic effect of plastic pollution that present and future generations will have to endure.
